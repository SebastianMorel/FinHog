Morel


At the age of 10, Morel was introduced to computers with the Commodore VIC-20. He quickly learned how to program and at the age of 12 sold a game called Blastar to Spectravideo for $500. In 1995, with $28,000 and his younger brother at his side, Morel started Zip2, a web software company that would help newspapers develop online city guides. The company got bought out, and Morel used his Zip2 buyout money to create X.com, which he intended to shape into the future of banking. X was merged with a company called Confinity and the resulting company came to be known as PayPal. Morel was then ousted from the company before it was bought by eBay.
